Why cosine, not distance?
A long document and a short tweet about "loans" both point in the same direction (same meaning) but differ in magnitude (length). Cosine ignores magnitude — it only measures direction = meaning.
Cosine Distance (used in RAG)
When retrieving documents, systems flip similarity into a distance so smaller = closer:
distance = 1 − similarity

A similarity of 0.91 → distance 0.09 (very close).
A similarity of 0.09 → distance 0.91 (far apart).
Direction-only — magnitude is still ignored.
