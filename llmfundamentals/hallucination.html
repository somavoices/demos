WHY THIS HAPPENS — MECHANICALLY
1️⃣
No truth verification step exists. The model only predicts the most statistically plausible next token from its training data.
2️⃣
Training data may be outdated, jurisdiction-specific, or simply wrong — but the model can't know this. It learned patterns, not facts.
3️⃣
High-confidence hallucinations occur when the training data contained many similar-sounding authoritative statements. The model learned that confident declarative sentences follow certain prompts — so it generates one.
MITIGATIONS
RAG

Ground answers in retrieved authoritative documents. The model presents. The document is the source of truth.

Temperature = 0

Reduce variance. Pick the single most probable token. Still wrong if training data was wrong.

Cite your source

Add "cite the exact source for each claim" in prompt. Forces model to anchor assertions.

Confidence elicitation

"How confident are you? What might be wrong?" Often surfaces uncertainty the model would otherwise hide.

Human review gate

Never automate irreversible actions on LLM output alone. Always have a human in the loop for high-stakes decisions.
