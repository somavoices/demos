RAG Chunking Strategies

// Before you can search documents, you have to slice them. How you slice changes everything.
SOURCE TEXT: DBS Personal Loan policy — split every 100 tokens, ignoring sentence/paragraph boundaries
The DBS Personal Loan offers competitive interest rates starting from 3.88% p.a. Customers may borrow between S$1,000 and S$200,000. Loan tenure ranges from 1 to 5 years. Monthly repayments are fixed throughout the loan period. Early repayment is allowed with a fee of 1% of outstanding balance. Applicants must be Singapore Citizens or PRs aged 21 to 65 with a minimum annual income of S$20,000. Self-employed applicants require 2 years of tax records. The application process takes 3–5 business days. Approval is subject to credit assessment. All loans are subject to DBS lending guidelines and MAS regulations on responsible borrowing.
✓ PROS
  • Simple to implement
  • Predictable token count
  • Fast processing
✗ CONS
  • Splits mid-sentence ("Early | repayment")
  • Breaks logical units arbitrarily
  • Poor semantic coherence per chunk
WHEN TO USE
Only for quick prototypes or when text has no natural structure (raw logs, telemetry). Never for policy docs, contracts, or FAQ content.
SOURCE TEXT: Same loan policy — split at every sentence boundary using NLP sentence detection
The DBS Personal Loan offers competitive interest rates starting from 3.88% p.a. Customers may borrow between S$1,000 and S$200,000. Loan tenure ranges from 1 to 5 years. Monthly repayments are fixed throughout the loan period. Early repayment is allowed with a fee of 1% of outstanding balance. Applicants must be Singapore Citizens or PRs aged 21 to 65 with a minimum annual income of S$20,000. Self-employed applicants require 2 years of tax records.
✓ PROS
  • Clean, complete sentences
  • Each chunk has a singular fact
  • High semantic coherence per chunk
✗ CONS
  • Loses cross-sentence context ("it" becomes ambiguous)
  • Very short chunks may embed poorly
  • Too granular for multi-part answers
WHEN TO USE
FAQ databases, policy rule extraction, compliance checking where each rule is a single sentence.
SOURCE TEXT: Same policy — split at paragraph/section boundaries (natural document structure preserved)
[Chunk 1 — Rates & Amounts] The DBS Personal Loan offers competitive interest rates starting from 3.88% p.a. Customers may borrow between S$1,000 and S$200,000. Loan tenure ranges from 1 to 5 years. Monthly repayments are fixed throughout the loan period. [Chunk 2 — Early Repayment] Early repayment is allowed with a fee of 1% of outstanding balance. Partial repayments are accepted in multiples of S$500. [Chunk 3 — Eligibility] Applicants must be Singapore Citizens or PRs aged 21 to 65 with a minimum annual income of S$20,000. Self-employed applicants require 2 years of tax records.
✓ PROS
  • Preserves natural topic coherence
  • Context within each chunk is complete
  • Works well for structured documents
✗ CONS
  • Paragraphs vary wildly in size
  • Requires clean document structure
  • PDF/scanned docs lose paragraph markers
WHEN TO USE
Well-structured Word docs, policy manuals, product descriptions — the most common production choice for banking documents.
STRATEGY: Fixed-size chunks with deliberate 25% overlap — each chunk shares tokens with its neighbours to prevent information loss at boundaries
The DBS Personal Loan offers rates from 3.88% p.a. Customers may borrow S$1,000–S$200,000. Tenure: 1–5 years. ← Chunk 1 (tokens 1–100)
OVERLAPTenure: 1–5 years. Monthly repayments fixed. Early repayment allowed with 1% fee of outstanding balance. ← Chunk 2 (tokens 75–175)
OVERLAP1% fee of outstanding balance. Applicants must be Citizens or PRs, aged 21–65, income ≥ S$20,000. ← Chunk 3 (tokens 150–250)
✓ PROS
  • No information lost at boundaries
  • Cross-boundary context preserved
  • Best for flowing narrative text
✗ CONS
  • Duplicate tokens → higher storage cost
  • May retrieve near-identical chunks
  • Need deduplication post-retrieval
WHEN TO USE
Annual reports, regulatory circulars, legal agreements — anywhere a key fact might sit at the boundary between two fixed chunks. Industry default: 20% overlap.
STRATEGY: Use embeddings to detect where the topic shifts — split there. Variable size, maximum semantic coherence.
[Topic: Loan Rates & Terms] Embedding similarity stays HIGH across sentences about rates, amounts, and tenure → grouped into one chunk automatically. ⚡ SEMANTIC BREAK DETECTED — cosine similarity drops below threshold (0.75) [Topic: Eligibility Criteria] Embedding similarity HIGH across sentences about age, citizenship, income → new chunk. ⚡ SEMANTIC BREAK DETECTED [Topic: Application Process] Processing time, credit check, approval conditions → new chunk.
✓ PROS
  • Highest semantic coherence of any strategy
  • Variable size adapts to content
  • Retrieval precision dramatically improves
✗ CONS
  • Computationally expensive to build
  • Requires tuning the similarity threshold
  • Not available in all RAG frameworks
WHEN TO USE
High-stakes retrieval (compliance, audit, customer-facing answers) where a missed retrieval has real consequences. Used by LlamaIndex and LangChain advanced RAG.
STRATEGY: Embed documents at multiple levels simultaneously — full document, section, paragraph, sentence. Retrieve at the right granularity.
Level 1 — Document Summary: "DBS Personal Loan: rates, eligibility, repayment, and application process." (1 embedding per doc) Level 2 — Section: "Loan Rates & Terms" | "Eligibility" | "Application Process" (1 embedding per section) Level 3 — Paragraph: Each paragraph embedded separately (standard chunks) Level 4 — Sentence: Each sentence embedded for fine-grained fact retrieval
✓ PROS
  • Right granularity for different queries
  • Broad questions hit doc-level; specific ones hit sentence-level
  • Best retrieval quality overall
✗ CONS
  • 4–5× more embeddings to store
  • Complex routing logic needed
  • Higher infrastructure cost
WHEN TO USE
Enterprise knowledge bases with diverse query types — "what is your loan policy?" (doc-level) vs. "what is the early repayment fee?" (sentence-level) need different granularity.
STRATEGY SCORECARD
Key insight
Wrong chunking is the #1 cause of RAG failure in production. A perfect LLM with bad chunks returns bad answers. The embedding model can only retrieve what's in the chunk — if the relevant sentence was split across two chunks, neither chunk will score high enough to be retrieved.
